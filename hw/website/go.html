South Beach: The Fillmore



The Fillmore Miami Beach at the Jackie Gleason Theater is a consistent mix of Miami Beach art deco and an awesome music environment. This legendary 1950 performance center is the ideal area to have your next corporate occasion, private show, grant function, mixed drink gathering, philanthropic event or conference. Its stupendous passageway, complete with a porte-cochere, a broad front yard, and 12 custom boxes, optimal for unique performances and events. Located next to the Miami Beach Convention Center, the Fillmore Miami Beach accommodates anywhere 5 to 2,700 guests.

Brickell: Lot 11 Skatepark



Lot 11 Skatepark is a newly opened 45,000 square foot skatepark under the I-95 in Government Center, Miami. The skate plaza will feature multiple skate areas catering to all skill levels and styles, including state-of-the-art contest courses, bowls and street sections, along with a flexible beginners area. This project will be the first of its kind in the South Florida region, and is the first public skatepark in Miami. If you're in Brickell, be sure to head over to Andrew Downtown to pick up a skateboard or just head over to Lot 11 to watch all the action.

Wynwood: Wynwood Walls



The Wynwood Walls was founded by the respected community revitalizer and developer, the late Tony Goldman in 2009. He arrived with a simple, unique idea: “Wynwood’s large stock of warehouse buildings, all with no windows, would be my giant canvases to bring to them the greatest street art ever seen in one place.” The Wynwood Walls became a major art statement with Tony’s commitment to graffiti and street art, a genre that he believed was under appreciated and not respected historically.
