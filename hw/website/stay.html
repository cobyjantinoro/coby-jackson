Where to Stay in Miami

Stay in the city or on the beach.

Miami offers tons of fun and elegant places to stay ranging from city high-rise hotels in Brickell to beach front rooms in Miami Beach. Three of Miami's best hotels from it's top cities are the 1 Hotel South Beach, The Mandarin Oriental in Brickell, and the Hyde Midtown Miami in Wynwood.


South Beach: 1 Hotel




Website: 1 Hotel South Beach
2341 Collins Ave, Miami Beach, FL 33139
(305) 604-1000

1 Hotel South Beach Miami is designed for comfort using reclaimed materials and is one-of-a-kind. This upscale beachside destination is a mile from outdoor shopping on Lincoln Road and only a 3 minutes' walk from the Bass Museum of Art. The 1 boasts over 600 feet of linear beachfront, 4 outdoor swimming pools (the largest on the roof), and a few restaurants and bars including an organic dining option by Tom Colicchio.

Brickell: Mandarin Oriental




Website: Mandarin Oriental

500 Brickell Key Dr, Miami, FL 33131
(305) 913-8288

Surrounded by stunning water and city views, Mandarin Oriental, Miami brings the essence of Five-Star luxury to Brickell Key. With a private infinity edge pool, high energy bayside dining and an exotic day spa, the hotel offers a taste of Asian serenity in sun-kissed Miami. This luxurious bayside hotel is a mile from Bayfront Park and 2 miles from sports and entertainment at the American Airlines Arena. There is also stylish restaurants offering Peruvian and modern American cuisine, an upscale spa, and a private beach.

Wynwood: Hyde Midtown Miami




Website: Hyde Midtown Miami

101 NE 34th St, Miami, FL 33137
(786) 899-5300

Wynwood has quickly become Miami's hottest, most hip neighborhood with its ecclectic, energetic art hub atmosphere full of life, and you’ll find Hyde Midtown Miami right in the heart of it. So all the best of the city’s arts, culture, entertainment, restaurants—and vibrant outdoor murals—are all around you. Among shops and casual eateries, this chic all-suite hotel in a high-rise building is a mile from both Interstate 195 and the street art at Wynwood Walls.
